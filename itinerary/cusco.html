N°05 Peru Five days

Cusco

13.5320° S — 71.9675° W

Five days at altitude — coca tea and deliberately slow first steps, the Sacred Valley’s terraces and granaries, and one dawn above the clouds at the citadel itself.

The itinerary

I

The brief

Cusco is a city built twice: Inca masonry to shoulder height, everything since stacked on top. Both layers are load-bearing. Acclimatize, then look up.

Altitude writes the first rule of this itinerary: arrive, slow down, and let the city come to you for a day. Cusco sits at 3,400 meters and enforces respect; the reward for patience is that everything afterward — the valley, the ruins, the citadel — feels earned rather than endured.

The route descends before it climbs: a night in the Sacred Valley, six hundred meters lower, puts you closer to the Machu Picchu trains and kinder to your lungs. May is the seam between wet and dry seasons — green valleys, clear mornings, and the crowds not yet at full strength.

II

Day by day

Day 01 of 5

Eleven thousand feet

Land, breathe, and do almost nothing well. San Blas — the whitewashed artisans’ quarter above the plaza — is the right speed for a first day: balconies, coca tea, and one gentle loop past the cathedral and the twelve-angled stone.

  • 10:00 Touch down at 3,400 meters. Walk like you mean it slowly.
  • 12:00 Coca tea on a San Blas balcony, red roofs below.
  • 16:00 Plaza de Armas, the cathedral, the twelve-angled stone.
  • 19:00 Soup, an early night, and no pisco yet.

Day 02 of 5

The Sacred Valley

Over the pass and down into the Urubamba valley, where the Incas farmed the mountainsides in staircases. Pisac’s terraces pour off the ridge like contour lines made physical; Ollantaytambo, at the valley’s far end, still runs on its original streets and water channels.

  • 08:00 Over the pass — the valley opens two thousand feet below.
  • 09:30 Pisac’s terraces and granaries, condors optional.
  • 12:30 Market lunch: choclo, salt from the Maras pans.
  • 15:00 Ollantaytambo — a living town on Inca plumbing.
  • 18:00 Sleep in the valley, 600 meters kinder than Cusco.

Day 03 of 5

The citadel

The first train slides down the Urubamba gorge in the dark, and by half past eight you are through the gate with the city terraced out below you and the clouds still deciding. Give the famous view its ten minutes, then spend the hours in the quiet corners the day-trippers skip.

  • 06:10 First train down the gorge, river loud beside the rails.
  • 08:00 Buses up the switchbacks through cloud forest.
  • 08:30 Through the gate — the city opens below, mist burning off.
  • 10:00 The quiet corners: Temple of the Condor, the quarry.
  • 12:30 Sun Gate lookback, the whole ridge in one frame.
  • 17:00 Rail back up the valley, legs done, mind loud.

Day 04 of 5

Stone and gold

Back in Cusco with legs and lungs now fluent, take the city properly: the zigzag megaliths of Sacsayhuamán above town, the Inca sun temple wearing its colonial church like a coat, and the San Pedro market’s fruit you cannot name. Tonight, finally, the pisco sour.

  • 09:00 Sacsayhuamán — stones the size of rooms, fitted dry.
  • 11:30 Qorikancha: Inca curves under baroque vaults.
  • 14:00 San Pedro market, juice counter roulette.
  • 18:00 The first pisco sour, thoroughly deferred, thoroughly earned.

Day 05 of 5

Descent

A last slow morning in San Blas — coffee, one more balcony, one more look down the red-tiled bowl of the city — then the short flight out over the Andes, which look, from above, exactly as improbable as everything they held.

  • 08:00 Last coffee above the rooftops of San Blas.
  • 10:00 Down through the plaza once more, unhurried.
  • 12:30 Airborne over the Andes, forehead on the glass.

III — Field note

The masons cut stone to fit stone so exactly that a knife blade fails at the joints. Five hundred years of earthquakes have agreed to leave them alone.
IV

Essentials

Getting there

All roads run through Lima; the Cusco hop takes eighty minutes and morning flights dodge the weather. Trains to Machu Picchu leave from Ollantaytambo — book the citadel entry and the train weeks ahead, together.

Staying

San Blas for the views and the quiet, a valley lodge near Ollantaytambo for the citadel night. Both beat sleeping low on charm and high on oxygen, in that order.

Notes

Altitude is real: hydrate constantly, skip alcohol the first day, and keep coca tea within reach. Carry your passport for the citadel gate. Small bills move the markets.
