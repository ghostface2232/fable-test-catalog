N°06 France Four days

Paris

48.8566° N — 2.3522° E

Four days on the left bank and above the rooftops — museum mornings, covered passages, zinc and slate at dusk, and the river waiting at the end of every street.

The itinerary

I

The brief

Paris does not perform for visitors; it simply continues. The trick is to fall into step — counter coffee, long lunch, river at dusk — and let the city mistake you for a regular.

This is an itinerary of rhythms rather than sights. The monuments are all here, but sideways: the Louvre glimpsed through the Palais-Royal colonnades, the Eiffel Tower as a silhouette behind chimney pots, Notre-Dame from a bookseller’s green box on the quay. Paris rewards the oblique approach.

Base in Saint-Germain, courtyard side, and go everywhere on foot — two arrondissements a day is the honest maximum. Early October is the city’s best light: low, amber, arriving late enough that nobody reasonable misses a sunrise.

II

Day by day

Day 01 of 4

The river first

Begin where the city began. The quays from Pont Neuf to the Île Saint-Louis are Paris’s front hallway: booksellers closing their green boxes, lamps coming on in pairs, the river folding the whole sky into itself. Dinner is a bistro with a chalkboard and no translation.

  • 15:00 Bags down in Saint-Germain, window over the courtyard.
  • 16:30 Pont Neuf and the quays, downstream side first.
  • 18:00 The bouquinistes shutting their boxes, one by one.
  • 20:30 Bistro dinner: chalkboard, carafe, no hurry.

Day 02 of 4

Museum morning

The Orsay at opening bell, and straight to the fifth floor before the room fills — the impressionists in morning light, which is what they painted in the first place. Then the gardens: Tuileries chairs, green and immovable, and the hush of the Palais-Royal arcades.

  • 09:00 Musée d’Orsay at opening — fifth floor first.
  • 12:00 Lunch behind the great clock face, city through the numerals.
  • 14:00 A Tuileries chair, angled to the fountain, one hour minimum.
  • 16:00 Palais-Royal colonnades and the striped columns.
  • 19:00 An early film in a Left Bank cinéma, any one will do.

Day 03 of 4

Uphill, then over the rooftops

Montmartre before the easels arrive is still a village: the vineyard, the water-carrier’s street, laundry in the windows. Come down through the covered passages, then finish high — an apartment-floor window over the zinc rooftops as the evening light does its slow amber work.

  • 08:30 Rue de l’Abreuvoir and the vineyard, no one about.
  • 11:00 Down through the passages — Panoramas, then Vivienne.
  • 15:00 A high window in the 9th: zinc, chimney pots, haze.
  • 18:00 Wine bar below, the day going gold outside.

Day 04 of 4

Market, then out

Rue Mouffetard does its market theatre every morning and expects you to participate: cheese for the train, fruit for the walk, opinions for free. One last espresso standing at the counter — the only correct posture — and out through the Gare du Nord.

  • 08:00 Mouffetard market — cheese counsel taken seriously.
  • 10:30 Last espresso, standing, saucer rattling on zinc.
  • 12:00 Gare du Nord, north light through the glass roof.

III — Field note

The city rehearses its evening the way it has for two hundred years — and lets you watch, provided you order something.
IV

Essentials

Getting there

Eurostar straight into Gare du Nord, or CDG and the RER B. Load a carnet onto a Navigo pass and then ignore it; the walking is the point.

Staying

Saint-Germain or the lower 9th, courtyard side, always — the street side is romance until 2 a.m., plumbing after. A sixth-floor walk-up buys the rooftop view.

Notes

Museums close Monday or Tuesday, never both — check before promising yourself the Orsay. Dinner starts at 19:30 and not before. October asks for a coat and repays you in light.
