N°03 Morocco Four days

Merzouga

31.0994° N — 4.0084° W

Four days at the edge of the Sahara — a mountain road that ends in sand, a camel’s slow gait into the erg, one night under more stars than sky, and a silence you can hear.

The itinerary

I

The brief

Where the road gives out, the Erg Chebbi begins: a sea of dunes ten miles long, rising to five hundred feet, changing color on the hour. You go in on a camel and come out recalibrated.

The desert does not do itineraries; it does thresholds. This route is a sequence of them — over the Atlas, down the Ziz valley’s river of palms, past the last town, beyond the last streetlight — each one stripping something away until what is left is sand, light and time.

October is the month: the summer furnace has passed, the nights are cold enough for wool blankets and clear enough for the Milky Way. One night in a camp deep in the erg is the hinge of the whole journey; the kasbah at the dune line handles the nights on either side.

II

Day by day

Day 01 of 4

The road in

The drive from Fes is the overture: up through cedar forests where the macaques watch the road, over the Middle Atlas, and down into the Ziz valley, where a million palms follow the water through rust-colored rock. Late in the day the horizon grows a strange pink ridge — those are the dunes.

  • 08:00 Out of Fes, climbing into the cedars.
  • 10:30 Azrou’s forest — Barbary macaques at the treeline.
  • 13:00 Over the pass; lunch in Midelt, apples and tagine.
  • 15:30 The Ziz gorge unrolls its palmery below the road.
  • 18:00 First sight of Erg Chebbi, embers on the horizon.

Day 02 of 4

Into the erg

Morning belongs to the villages at the sand’s edge — Khamlia, where gnawa musicians play a music built from the desert’s own patience. In the late afternoon you mount up and ride into the dunes as the light goes long, arriving at camp with the last of it.

  • 06:30 Sunrise from the kasbah roof, dunes going rose then gold.
  • 10:00 Khamlia — gnawa rhythms, tea, iron castanets.
  • 17:00 Mount up. The camels lean into the first slope.
  • 18:40 Camp: wool tents in a dune bowl, fire already lit.
  • 21:00 Tagine, mint tea, drums, and the cold coming down.

Day 03 of 4

The dune at dawn

Climb the big dune in the dark — two steps up, one sliding back — and be on the crest when the sun clears Algeria. The rest of the day is deliberately empty: ride out, wash the sand off, sleep, and spend the evening on the kasbah roof learning the sky.

  • 05:30 Up the crest by starlight, breath loud in the silence.
  • 06:40 Sunrise. The erg switches on, dune by dune.
  • 08:00 Breakfast at camp; the camels are unimpressed.
  • 10:00 Ride out of the sand, back across the threshold.
  • 13:00 Hammam, then the deepest nap of the year.
  • 21:00 The roof, a blanket, a star chart, no hurry.

Day 04 of 4

Out with the light

One more sunrise if you can manage it, then the long road back — or the short flight from Errachidia, watching the erg shrink to a pink smudge and trying to explain to yourself how big it felt from inside.

  • 06:00 Final sunrise from the roof, coffee cooling fast.
  • 08:00 West through Rissani’s gates, the sand in your cuffs.
  • 12:00 The Atlas again, or the airstrip. Either way: out.

III — Field note

Distance in the erg is measured in silence — an hour of it, a night of it. You come back calibrated differently.
IV

Essentials

Getting there

Fly Casablanca → Errachidia and drive the last two hours, or make the full day’s road trip from Fes over the Middle Atlas — the drive is half the journey and worth doing in daylight.

Staying

A kasbah at the dune line for the first and last nights, and one night in a wool-tent camp deep in the erg. Ask for a camp without generators; the darkness is the point.

Notes

October nights fall near freezing — pack real layers. A cotton scarf earns its keep against sun and sand alike. Carry cash past Rissani; the last ATM does not believe in you.
