N°02 Italy Four days

Manarola

44.1064° N — 9.7276° E

Four days between vineyard and sea — trail dust on the high paths, harbor swims off the rocks, anchovies straight from the boat, and evenings the exact color of the Ligurian dusk.

The itinerary

I

The brief

The five villages were built by people who farmed a cliff. Walk the terraces first — everything else about the Cinque Terre makes sense from above.

This is a walking itinerary with swimming breaks, or possibly the reverse. One village a day is the right dose; the temptation to collect all five before lunch is how the magic gets spent. Manarola makes the best base — small enough to learn by heart, central enough to reach everything by trail, train or boat.

Come in late September. The water is still warm, the light has gone long and gold, and the sanctuary paths above the villages smell of harvest — crushed grapes riding down the cliffs on monorail carts.

II

Day by day

Day 01 of 4

Arrival by rail

The regional train threads tunnel after tunnel, each one ending in a flash of sea, and then you are simply there — deposited above a harbor the size of a courtyard. Drop the bags and get in the water before you unpack.

  • 14:00 The coastal line from La Spezia — sit on the sea side.
  • 15:30 First swim off the harbor rocks, boats swinging overhead.
  • 18:30 Punta Bonfiglio for the postcard view of your own village.
  • 20:00 Anchovies three ways, a carafe of the local white.

Day 02 of 4

The high path

Climb out of Manarola through the terraces to Volastra, then walk the shelf of vineyards to Corniglia with the sea five hundred feet below. This is the trail the postcards don’t show, and the best morning on this coast.

  • 08:00 Up the stone stairs into the vineyards while the air is cool.
  • 09:00 Volastra’s sanctuary — the coast laid out end to end.
  • 11:30 Into Corniglia, the one village the sea can’t reach.
  • 13:00 Long lunch on a terrace, no plans until it ends.
  • 16:30 Train home, gelato, the day’s second swim.

Day 03 of 4

By boat

The villages were built to be reached from the water, and the ferry restores the original point of view — five settlements jammed into the cliff’s few weaknesses. Land in Vernazza, climb the castle tower, and give the afternoon to pesto, which is a religion here, not a sauce.

  • 09:30 Ferry down the coast — the cliff face reads like a map.
  • 11:00 Vernazza: the piazza on the water, the Doria tower above it.
  • 14:00 A pesto lesson — basil, pine nuts, patience, marble.
  • 18:00 Riomaggiore’s harbor at golden hour, feet over the edge.
  • 21:30 Back home. The harbor lights carry on without you.

Day 04 of 4

Harvest light

A last slow morning: coffee on the marina, then up into the vines one more time to watch the harvest come down the cliff before the train carries you out along the same bright tunnels that brought you in.

  • 07:00 Espresso standing up, fishermen mending nets below.
  • 08:30 Into the terraces — September grapes, monorail carts.
  • 10:30 One last look from Punta Bonfiglio, memorize it.
  • 12:00 Train out, sea side again.

III — Field note

Distance here is vertical. The sea is always a hundred stairs away, and every one of them is worth it.
IV

Essentials

Getting there

Fly to Genoa or Pisa, then rail to La Spezia and the five-village local line. The Cinque Terre Card covers the trains between villages and the walking trails in one ticket.

Staying

A small room above Manarola’s harbor — book months ahead for September. Pack light: every address in the village involves stairs, and porters are a myth.

Notes

Swim shoes earn their keep on the rock beaches. The high trails close after heavy rain — check the boards at the station. Dinner without a reservation is a standing appointment with disappointment in season.
